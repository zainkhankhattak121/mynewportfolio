Overview

VeriFy — AI-Powered Fake News Detection

VeriFy is a full-stack web platform that uses BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) to detect whether a news article or claim is real or fake — with a confidence score on every result. It also cross-checks claims against four live news APIs for source-level verification, giving users two independent ways to fact-check content.

Core Features

BERT Fake News Detection — Paste any news text and the AI instantly labels it Real or Fake with a percentage confidence score (e.g. 100% Fake).
Live Source Verification — Cross-checks claims against Google Fact Check, NewsAPI, GNews, and MediaStack in real time.
Two Detection Modes — Switch between the AI Verification Model (BERT) and the Detection Model depending on the task.
Verification History — Full log of every past check with date, result badge, and confidence. Supports search, label filters, date range, and confidence range filters.
Analytics Dashboard — Live stats: total verifications, fake count, real count, and average confidence across all history.
Collections — Save and organise important verification results into named collections for later reference.
Export Options — Download full history as CSV or JSON for research, journalism, or personal records.

Built for journalists, researchers, students, and anyone who wants to quickly verify news before sharing — helping fight misinformation at scale.
